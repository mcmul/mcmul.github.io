Morrisons

June 20th, 2021

Private equity are coming for Morrisons, from The FT:

Britain’s fourth-largest supermarket chain Wm Morrison said on Saturday evening that it had rejected an unsolicited £8.7bn takeover bid from US private equity group Clayton, Dubilier & Rice.

It's easy to see why, just look at this line in their last annual report:

The balance sheet is very strong, both absolute and relative, 87% of our sites are freehold and the pension schemes are in a net £718m surplus position. Underlying debt is low, we have a strong maturity profile and, fundamentally, Morrisons is a very cash generative business.

To summarise: (1) they maintain a majority freehold portfolio, (2) they have low debt, and (3) they are cash generative. What's not to like?

The bid of 230p per share is a 29% premium to Friday's closing price of 178p:

bid: 230p outstanding shares: 2.41bn market value: £5.5bn net debt: 3.2bn ------ 8.7bn

Sadly, one knows exactly how this ends. From equitydealer in the FT comments:

Let me guess, they will sell off their stores and leverage the company to the limit before floating it back on the stock market in a few years.

And Dr Watt's Library in response:

Replace the “floating back on the stock exchange” with “put through a CVA at the next recession” and I think you’ve nailed it!

They're not wrong:

roughly 20 percent of large companies acquired through leveraged buyouts go bankrupt within ten years, as compared to a control group’s bankruptcy rate of 2 percent during the same time period

Let's not make them the next Debenhams, their customers and employees deserve better.
